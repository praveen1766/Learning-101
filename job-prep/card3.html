Card 3: Preparing for an Interview

An interview is your chance to shine. It's like meeting respected elders in your village – you want to be confident, polite, and show them your best self. Good preparation is key to success.

1. Research the Company

Story: Before the interview, find out what the company does. If it's an IT company in Hyderabad, what services do they offer? This shows you are genuinely interested.

Tip: Look at the company's website for 10-15 minutes.

2. Practice Common Questions

Story: Be ready to answer questions like "Tell me about yourself" and "Why do you want this job?". Practice your answers with a friend. This builds confidence.

Tip: Prepare a short, positive story about your skills.

3. Dress Professionally

Story: Wear clean, ironed, and formal or semi-formal clothes. It shows you are serious and respect the opportunity.

Analogy: Just like dressing well for a festival or a wedding in your village.

4. During the Interview

Story: Be polite, listen carefully, and maintain eye contact. At the end, thank the interviewer for their time. It leaves a good impression.

Tip: It's okay to say "I don't know, but I am eager to learn."

Key Technical Terms

  • Common Questions: Standard questions asked in most interviews (e.g., "What are your strengths?").
  • Body Language: How you present yourself non-verbally (e.g., sitting up straight, smiling).
  • Professionalism: Acting in a serious, respectful, and appropriate manner for a work environment.
  • Company Culture: The values and behaviors of a company. Researching this helps you fit in.
  • Follow-up: Sending a thank-you email after the interview. This is a very good practice.
