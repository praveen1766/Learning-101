Card 1: What is a Computer?

Imagine you're in a small village in Andhra Pradesh. Years ago, people used paper and pens for everything – writing letters, calculating rice prices, or keeping track of family records. Today, a computer does all that faster and easier, like a smart helper that never gets tired!

The Brain (CPU)

Story: Just like your brain thinks and solves problems, the CPU is the computer's brain. It processes information super fast – faster than counting grains of rice by hand!

Fun Fact: In Andhra Pradesh, farmers use computers to calculate crop yields quickly.

The Eyes (Monitor)

Story: The monitor is like the computer's eyes and mouth. It shows you pictures, words, and videos, so you can see what the computer is "saying".

Fun Fact: Watch Telugu movies or news from Hyderabad on it!

The Hands (Keyboard)

Story: The keyboard lets you "talk" to the computer. Type letters, numbers, and commands, just like writing on paper but digital.

Fun Fact: Use it to type in Telugu script for local stories.

The Pointer (Mouse)

Story: The mouse is like a magic wand. Move it to point at things on the screen, and click to select or open them.

Fun Fact: Click to play games or browse Andhra Pradesh tourism sites.

Key Technical Terms

  • Computer: An electronic device that processes data to perform tasks, like a super-smart calculator and storyteller.
  • CPU (Central Processing Unit): The brain of the computer that handles calculations and decisions.
  • Monitor: The screen that displays information, like a TV for the computer.
  • Keyboard: A device with keys for typing text and commands.
  • Mouse: A pointing device used to interact with items on the screen.
