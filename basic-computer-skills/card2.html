Card 2: Turning On and Off a Computer

Starting a computer is like waking up a sleeping elephant. In Andhra Pradesh, it's similar to starting a tractor for plowing fields – simple steps to get it ready for work.

Check the Power

Story: Make sure the computer is plugged in. Press the power button on the CPU or monitor.

Fun Fact: Like plugging in a fan before switching it on in a hot Andhra summer.

Wait for Startup

Story: The screen lights up, and you see the desktop. It takes a minute, like a bullock cart getting moving.

Fun Fact: In villages, computers boot up while you sip chai.

Shutting Down

Story: Click Start > Shut Down. Wait for it to turn off completely before unplugging.

Fun Fact: Always shut down properly to avoid "computer sickness," like resting after hard work.

Key Technical Terms

  • Power Button: The button that turns the computer on or off.
  • Boot Up: The process of starting the computer and loading the operating system.
  • Desktop: The main screen you see after logging in, with icons and shortcuts.
  • Shut Down: Properly turning off the computer to save energy and data.
  • Restart: Turning off and on again to fix minor issues.
