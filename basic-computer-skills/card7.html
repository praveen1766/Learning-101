Card 7: Basic Computer Troubleshooting

Sometimes, computers have problems. Troubleshooting is like being a detective to find and fix them. Just like when a farmer checks their tractor for issues, you can learn to solve simple computer problems yourself.

1. The Golden Rule: Restart It

Story: If your computer is slow or something isn't working right, the first and most effective step is often to simply restart it. This clears out many temporary problems.

Analogy: Giving a tired farm animal a fresh start after a good night's rest.

2. Program is Not Responding (Frozen)

Story: If an application freezes, you can force it to close. On Windows, press `Ctrl+Alt+Delete` to open the Task Manager. On a Mac, use `Option+Command+Esc`.

Tip: You can then select the frozen program and end the task.

3. Computer is Running Slow

Story: Having too many programs open at once can slow down your computer, like trying to carry too many mango baskets at once. Close any applications you are not using.

Tip: Also, make sure you have some free space on your computer's main drive.

4. Internet is Not Working

Story: If you can't connect to the internet, check the WiFi symbol on your computer. A simple fix is to restart your WiFi router (the small box from your internet provider). Unplug it, wait 30 seconds, and plug it back in.

Analogy: Checking if the main water valve is open before checking the taps.

Key Technical Terms

  • Troubleshooting: The process of identifying and fixing problems.
  • Restart/Reboot: Turning your computer off and then on again.
  • Freeze/Not Responding: When a program gets stuck and stops working.
  • Task Manager (Windows) / Force Quit (Mac): Tools to close frozen applications.
  • Router: The device that provides your home with an internet connection via WiFi.
